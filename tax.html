Income Tax Law Guide

Introduction Withholding Rates Calculation Example Responsibilities
Introduction

The Income Tax Law 2009 has modified some applications of the wage withholding tax. This guide outlines those changes and provides assistance in complying with the law.

Legal and natural persons with two or more employees during any month of the tax year must withhold tax from employees whose income exceeds the threshold discussed in Article 58 of the Income Tax Law.

Withholding Rates

Effective 1 Hamal 1388, the new tax law provides a monthly schedule of withholding rates based on income brackets:

  • Income up to AFN 5,000: 0%
  • Income between AFN 5,000 - 12,500: 2% of amount over 5,000 AFN
  • Income between AFN 12,500 - 100,000: 150 AFN + 10% over 12,500 AFN
  • Income over AFN 100,000: 8,900 AFN + 20% over 100,000 AFN
Calculation Example

Example: Abdullah earns AFN 20,000 per month. His monthly withholding tax would be AFN 900 calculated as follows:

20,000 – 12,500 = 7,500

7,500 × 10% (0.1) = 750

750 + 150 = 900 AFN tax

Net salary: 20,000 - 900 = 19,100 AFN

Responsibilities

Employers must remit the withheld tax to the State no later than 10 days after the end of the month. Annual salary and tax statements must be provided to employees and forwarded to the Ministry of Finance.

Employees with a single job have no further filing requirements. Those with additional income must file an individual income tax return and can credit the withheld tax against their total tax liability.
